The Team

Aashna Shroff

Aashna Shroff is a sophomore at Stanford pursuing Computer Science and Economics. She's a strong believer in the power of applying computational tools to interdisciplinary problems. After being one of the only two girls in the computer science class in her high school in India, she was determined to bring an initiative like Girls Code Camp to India. At Stanford, her activities include advocating for diversity in the tech community, serving on the board for Stanford Women in Computer Science, and mentoring for groups like Girls Teaching Girls To Code and she++. She has a contagious love for learning and is excited to share it through GCC.

Find out more

John Kamalu

John Kamalu is a freshman interested in Computer Science and Linguistics at Stanford University, whose love of learning began with Classical Latin and Ancient Greek. In high school, he spent much of his time as an educational aid, whether as a peer tutor introductory Latin and Computer Science, or as a general tutor for local, underprivileged youth. More recently, aside from fulfilling Stanford’s Computer Science curriculum, he along with his team members placed Top-8 at TreeHacks, Stanford’s annual, international hackathon. He is excited to bring GCC to Hyderabad, and looks forward to exploring the art of teaching further.

Find out more

Akhila Moturu

Akhila Moturu is a freshmen majoring in Computer Science at Stanford University, seeking a minor in Education. On campus, she is currently involved in Women in Computer Science, Stanford Pre Education Society, and Dreamcatchers. In high school, she served as 1st Vice President of her Technology Student Association chapter and earned over 7 first place state awards in different competition categories in TSA. She is passionate about encouraging girls to pursue careers in the STEM fields.
